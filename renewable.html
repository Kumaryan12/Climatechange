The Power of Renewable Energy

Renewable energy is energy generated from natural resources—such as sunlight, wind, rain, tides, waves, and geothermal heat—that are replenished on a human timescale.

Solar Energy

Solar power is harnessed by capturing sunlight and converting it into electricity or thermal energy.

Wind Energy

Wind power is generated by converting the kinetic energy of wind into mechanical energy, which can be used to produce electricity.

Hydropower

Hydropower uses water flow to generate electricity, typically with the help of dams that control water flow.

Geothermal Energy

Geothermal power taps into the heat from below the Earth's surface to generate electricity and provide heating.

Biomass Energy

Biomass energy is produced from organic materials, including plant and animal waste, which are converted into fuel.

Tidal Energy

Tidal energy utilizes the movement of tides to generate electricity, offering a reliable renewable energy source.
