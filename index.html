    Goa doctor rejuvenates springs, lakes to combat climate change

    Goa is facing climate change impacts like coastline erosion, altered rainfall patterns, and disrupted ecosystems. Environmentalists, like Dr. Dattaram Desai, have been working on water conservation since 1991, while efforts by individuals like Rajendra Kerkar and Nitin Borkar focus on preserving biodiversity and combating plastic waste. Conservation measures such as planting trees and mangroves are being promoted to mitigate climate change effects.

    Project SAIM: A Milestone in Goa's Waste Management

    Goa has launched Project SAIM, a sustainable waste management initiative by WWF-India and Saahas Zero Waste, aimed at transforming waste handling in the state. The newly inaugurated Material Recovery Facility (MRF) in Nessai, Margao, processes up to 7.5 tonnes of dry waste daily and supports decentralized waste management in 30 South Goa panchayats. Project SAIM promotes recycling, co-processing, and sustainable organic waste management, while fostering community involvement. The initiative aims to position Goa as a leader in sustainable waste practices, contributing to biodiversity conservation and environmental stewardship.
